\section{How to win the game ?}

\aparag Even if the spirit of the game is mainly to simulate the whole modern
history, a complete victory points (\VPs) system is explain here. It allow to
designate a winner of the campaign, and also to know who is doing well and who
isn't. This \VP system is composed of 4 parts.
\bparag {\VPs per turn: }
Those \VPs are earned by player performing special actions and discoveries. It
corresponds approximately to the quarter of the final \VPs.
\bparag{End of period \VPs: }
At the end of each period, players earn \VPs corresponding to their situations
and to the objectives they chose at the beginning of the period. It
corresponds approximately to the half of the final \VPs.  They receive also
\VPs according to their Wealth and Prestige Expenses during the past turns.
\bparag{End of game \VPs: }
At the end of the game, after counting \VPs of the end of seventh period, a
check-up of the the situation of each player is made, and \VPs are earned
according to.

\aparag[\VPs of minor powers]
The players also earn (or lose) half of the \VPs earned (or lost) by a minor
country which entered war at their side.

\aparag[Definitions of \terme{monopolies}]
\terme{Monopolies} are defined for each \STZ/\CTZ and for exploitation of each
exotics resources
\bparag \STZ/\CTZ : a player has a partial \terme{monopoly} in a \CTZ or a
\STZ when he is the only possesser of a face \Faceplus fleet counter in this
zone. He has a total \terme{monopoly} when he has a 6 levels fleet, and there
is no other fleet in the zone.
\bparag \label{chVictories:MonopolyExoticResources} Exploitation of exotic
resources : for each resource, a player has a partial \terme{monopoly} when he
produce at least 6 units and half of the world production of this resource. He
has a total \terme{monopoly} when he produce all units but 2 of this resource.




\section{End of period \VPs}

\aparag The end of period \VPs are decomposed in 3 parts: Wealth and Prestige
Expenses \VPs, a check up of the situation of each country and the
verification of the objectives chosen at the beginning of the period.

\aparag[Wealth and Prestige Expenses]
\VPs are accorder by taking the mean of Wealth (Gross Income + Prestige
Expenses) on the turns of the past period. This sum is multiplied by the
modifier given afterwards (depending on country and period), here expressed in
\%.
\bparag At the end of the game, or in case of transferal, add to the sum of
Wealth the Royal Treasury, else, if negative, remoce twice this RT.  Remove
also twice the amount of Loans not refunded.
\bparag All in all, one can expect to gain 100\VPs in average each period
(with modulations depending on the fastest periods of each power).

\aparag[Period's objectives]
\bparag At the beginning of each period, each player has a choice of 5
objectives in the lists detailed below, and he must secretly pick up 3 of
them. One of those 3 must be marked as the prioritary one, the 2 other would
be the non-prioritary ones.
\bparag At the end of the period, objectives are checked, and players earn
\VPs according to this. A \VP value is indicated in the tables for each
objectives. If a player choose an objective and complete it, he earn its \VP
value, if it is the prioritary one, he earn twice this value.
\bparag Some objectives are marked on the tables with {\bf M}, they are
objectives with malus, if a players don't pick up this one, and don't complete
it, he loses the \VP value of this one.
\bparag Some objectives are marked on the tables with {\bf E}. They are
objectives which depend on the occurence of an event. If the corresponding
event has not occurred when the period ends, the check for the success of the
objective is postponed until the event occurs and terminates.  If the event
can not happen anymore (due to being 2 periods before), then the objective is
considered as being half realised, entitling the player the gain of half the
VP value.
\bparag[MNU] Some objectives are explained as MNU: those objectives consist in
having at the end of the period a number of Manufacture units strictly
superior to the period limit.
\bparag[Domination commercial in \STZ{Baltique}]
Having Commercial Monopoly in \STZ{Baltique} (with no one levying Taxes), or
Levying Taxes on the Sund, or having diplomatic control of a minor country
having the Rights of Levies on the Sund (raising them or not), see
\ruleref{chSpecific:Sund Levies}.
\bparag[Dominium Marii Baltici] (Swedish objective) Owning all provinces
bordering the \region{Baltique}, excepted for provinces currently owned by
\pays{Brandebourg} (or \PRU) or \pays{Danemark}.

\section{End of game \VPs}\label{chVictories:End Game}

\aparag Those \VPs consist of a check up of the situation of each players at
the end of the game. A check up of the same kind, which is also explained here
is done each time a player change of played country.
\aparag The objectives are exclusive, it means that if a provinces is listed
in a special objective for a country, it does not count for the ``all
non-national province'' objective.
\aparag The provinces of \VASSAL minor count for special objectives but not
for ``all non-national province'' objectives.
\aparag Exotic resources count for \COL and \TP income, but not the gold.
\aparag The verification for the rank of army or fleet are made at the
beginning of the last turn and not at the end and are counted respectivly in
\LD ans in \NWD.
